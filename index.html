About Me

Security Engineer

I make detections
that fire on real threats.

I spent three years at IBM doing the unglamorous work that actually keeps enterprise security running — chasing down QRadar service failures at odd hours, integrating 150+ log sources that were "already onboarded" according to someone's ticket, and writing detection rules precise enough to fire on real threats without burying analysts in noise. That kind of environment teaches you to care about signal quality above everything else.

The results were measurable: 35% drop in Mean Time to Detect, 45% fewer false positives, 99.99% SIEM uptime across clients processing up to 50,000 events per second.

Now I'm pushing into AI security — an area most teams are still figuring out. I built ThreatSight to auto-generate MITRE-mapped Sigma rules using LLM agents, and Sapphire Sentinel to stress-test agentic AI against prompt injection. Neither was assigned. Both came from watching a gap and deciding to fill it.

Outside of work I run the Transamerica Cybersecurity Student Club at CU Denver — 450+ members, hands-on workshops, real industry connections.
